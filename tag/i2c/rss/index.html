Odd Bitshttp://blog-beta.oddbit.com/One of these things is not like the othersTue, 12 Mar 2013 00:00:00 -0400I2C on the Raspberry Pihttp://blog-beta.oddbit.com/posts/2013/03/12/i2c-on-the-raspberry/<p>I've set up my <a href="http://www.raspberrypi.org/">Raspberry Pi</a> to communicate with my <a href="http://www.arduino.cc/">Arduino</a> via <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I%C2%B2C">I2C</a>. The Raspberry Pi is a 3.3v device and the Arduino is a 5v device. While in general this means that you need to use a level converter when connecting the two devices, <strong>you don't need to use a level converter when connecting the Arduino to the Raspberry Pi via I2C.</strong> </p> <p>The design of the I2C bus is such that the only device driving a voltage on the bus is the master (in this case, the Raspberry Pi), via pull-up resistors. So when "idle", the bus is pulled to 3.3v volts by the Pi, which is perfectly safe for the Arduino (and compatible with it's 5v signaling). To transmit data on the bus, a device brings the bus low by connecting it to ground. In other words, slave devices <em>never</em> drive the bus high. This means that the Raspberry Pi will never see a 5v signal from the Arduino...unless, of course, you make a mistake and accidentally <code>digitalWrite</code> a <code>HIGH</code> value on one of the Arduino's <code>I2C</code> pins. So don't do that.</p> <p>Note that the built-in pull-up resistors are <em>only</em> available on the Pi's I2C pins (Pins 3 (<code>SDA</code>) and 5 (<code>SCL</code>), aka BCM <code>GPIO0</code> and <code>GPIO1</code> on a Rev. 1 board, <code>GPIO2</code> and <code>GPIOP3</code> on a Rev. 2 board):</p> <p><img alt="Raspberry Pi Pins" src="/assets/raspberry-pi-i2c-pins.jpg" /></p> <p>On the Arduino Uno, the <code>I2C</code> pins are pins <code>A4</code> (<code>SDA</code>) and <code>A5</code> (<code>SCL</code>):</p> <p><img alt="Arduino Uno Pins" src="/assets/arduino-i2c-pins.jpg" /></p> <p>For information about other boards and about the Arduino I2C API, see the documentation for the <a href="http://arduino.cc/en/Reference/Wire">Wire library</a>.</p>Lars Kellogg-StedmanTue, 12 Mar 2013 00:00:00 -0400tag:blog-beta.oddbit.com,2013-03-12:posts/2013/03/12/i2c-on-the-raspberry/hardwareraspberrypiarduinoi2c
